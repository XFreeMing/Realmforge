
1  海外南经:
地之所载，六合之间，四海之内，照之以日月，经之以星辰，纪之以四时，要之以太岁，神灵所生，其物异形，或夭或寿，唯圣人能通其道。
What the earth supports, within the 六合 (six directions), within the four seas, is illuminated by the sun and moon, regulated by stars and constellations, marked by the four seasons, and governed by the Tai sui. Spirits and deities are born there; their forms vary greatly, some short-lived and others long-lived. Only a sage can comprehend their principles.
2  海外南经:
海外自西南陬，至东南陬者。
From the southwest corner of the outer seas to the southeast corner.
3  海外南经:
结匈国在其西南，其为人结匈。
The Jiehungguo is located to its southwest, and the people there have joined chests.
4  海外南经:
南山在其东南。自此山来，虫为蛇，蛇号为鱼。一曰南山在结匈东南。
Nanshan Mountain lies to its southeast. From this mountain, creatures become snakes, and snakes are called fish. One account says that Nanshan Mountain is located to the southeast of Jiehungguo.
5  海外南经:
比翼鸟在其东，其为鸟青、赤，两鸟比翼。一曰在南山东。
The Biyiniao (Biyi bird) lies to its east; these birds are blue and red in color, and two of them fly with joined wings. One account says it is located to the east of Nanshan Mountain.
6  海外南经:
羽民国在其东南，其为人长头，身生羽。一曰在比翼鸟东南，其为人长颊。
The Yuminguo lies to its southeast; the people there have long heads and grow feathers on their bodies. One account says it is located in the southeast of Biyiniao, where the people have long cheeks.
7  海外南经:
有神人二八，连臂，为帝司夜于此野。在羽民东。其为人小颊赤肩。尽十六人。
There are sixteen divine beings who hold hands, serving as officials for the emperor to oversee the night in this wilderness. It lies to the east of Yuminguo. The people there have narrow cheeks and red shoulders. All sixteen of them.
8  海外南经:
毕方鸟在其东，青水西，其为鸟人面一脚。一曰在二八神东。
The Bifangniao lies to its east, west of the Qing Shui; this bird has a human face and one leg. One account says it is located east of the sixteen divine beings.
9  海外南经:
欢头国在其南，其为人人面有翼，鸟喙，方捕鱼。一曰在毕方东。或曰欢朱国。
The Huantouguo lies to its south; the people there have human faces and wings, bird beaks, and are fishing. One account says it is located east of Bifangniao. Some say it is the Huanzhu Guo.
10  海外南经:
厌火国在其国南，兽身黑色，生火出其口中。一曰在欢朱东。
The Yanhuoguo lies to the south of this country; its people have beast-like bodies, are black in color, and emit fire from their mouths. One account says it is located eastward of Huantouguo (or Huanzhuguo).
11  海外南经:
株树在厌火北，生赤水上，其为树如柏，叶皆为珠。一曰其为树若彗。
The Zhushu lies to the north of Yanhuoguo, growing along the Chishui; this tree resembles a cypress, and its leaves are all pearls. One account says the tree looks like a comet.
12  海外南经:
苗国在赤水东，其为人相随。一曰三毛国。
Miaoguo lies to the east of Chishui; its people follow one another. One account says it is Sanmaoguo.
13  海外南经:
臷国在其东，其为人黄，能操弓射蛇。一曰臷国在三毛东。
Zhiguo lies to its east; the people there are yellow in color and can handle bows to shoot snakes. One account says Zhiguo is located east of Sanmaoguo.
14  海外南经:
贯匈国在其东，其为人匈有窍。一曰在臷国东。
Guanxionguo lies to its east; the people have a hole in their chests. One account says it is located eastwards of Zhiguo.
15  海外南经:
交胫国在其东，其为人交胫。一曰在穿匈东。
Jiaojingguo lies to its east, and the people there have crossed shins. One account says it is located eastern of Guanxionguo.
16  海外南经:
不死民在其东，其为人黑色，寿，不死。一曰在穿匈国东。
The Busimguo lies to its east; its people are black in color and long-lived, never dying. One account says it is located eastof Guanxionguo (Guanxiu).
17  海外南经:
岐舌国在其东。一曰在不死民东。
Qisheguo lies to its east. One account says it is located Busimguo.
18  海外南经:
昆仑墟在其东，墟四方。一曰在岐舌东，为墟四方。
Kunlun Xu lies to its east, with a square-shaped site. One account says it is located Qisheguo, where the site is square in shape.
19  海外南经:
羿与凿齿战于寿华之野，羿射杀之。在昆仑墟东。羿持弓矢，凿齿持盾。一曰戈。
Yi and Zuochi fought on the plains of Shouhua, where Yi shot and killed him. It is located to the east of Kunlun Xu. Yi held a bow and arrow, while Zuochi held a shield. One account says it was a halberd.
20  海外南经:
首国在其东，其为人一身三首。一曰在凿齿东。
Shouguo lies to its east; its inhabitants have one body with three heads. One account says it is locatedeast of Zuochi's territory.
21  海外南经:
周饶国在其东，其为人短小，冠带。一曰焦侥国在三首东。
Zhouraoguo lies to its east; their people are short in stature and wear hats and sashes. One account says Jiaojioguo is located to the east of Sishouguo (the land of three heads).
22  海外南经:
长臂国在其东，捕鱼水中，两手各操一鱼。一曰在焦侥东，捕鱼海中。
Changbiguo lies to its east, where people fish in water, each holding a fish with both hands. One account says it is locatedto the east of Jiaojioguo, fishing in the sea.
23  海外南经:
狄山，帝尧葬于阳，帝喾葬于阴。爰有熊、罴、文虎、蜼、豹、离朱、视肉。吁咽、文王皆葬其所。一曰汤山。一曰爰有熊、罴、文虎、蜼、豹、离朱、𩿨久、视肉、宓交。其范林方三百里。
Dishan Mountain: Emperor Yao was buried on its southern side, and Emperor Ku was buried on its northern side. There are also bears, brown bears, tigers with patterns, langurs, leopards, Lizhu, and Shimrou. Yu Yan and King Wen were both buried there. One account says it is Tangshan Mountain. One account says there are also bears, brown bears, patterned tigers, langurs, leopards,Lizhu, Xijiu, Shimrou, and Hujiao. Its Fanlin forest extends for three hundred li in each direction.
24  海外南经:
南方祝融，兽身人面，乘两龙。
In the south is Zhurong, who has a beast-like body and a human face, riding two dragons.
