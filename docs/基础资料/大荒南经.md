5 打开字典显示相似段落	大荒南经:	
大荒之中，有不庭之山，荣水穷焉。有人三身，帝俊妻娥皇，生此三身之国，姚姓，黍食，使四鸟。有渊四方，四隅皆达，北属黑水，南属大荒，北旁名曰少和之渊，南旁名曰从渊，舜之所浴也。
In the great wilderness, there is a mountain called Buting; the Rong Shui River ends there. There are people with three bodies. Di Jun's wife, Ehuang, gave birth to this nation of tripartite beings. They bear the surname Yao, eat millet, and command four birds. There is a square-shaped abyss, with its four corners all connected. To the north it connects to Hei Shui and to the south to the great wilderness. The northern side is called Shaohuo Yuan, while the southern side is named Congyuan; this was where Shun bathed.

14 打开字典显示相似段落	大荒南经:	
有臷民之国。帝舜生无淫，降臷处，是谓巫臷民。巫臷民肦姓，食谷，不绩不经，服也；不稼不穑，食也。爰有歌舞之鸟，鸾鸟自歌，凤鸟自舞。爰有百兽，相群爰处。百谷所聚。
There is a country called Zhimin. Emperor Shun gave birth to Wuyin, who descended and settled there; these people are known as the Wuzhi Min. The Wuzhi Min bear the surname Zhan, eat grains, do not spin or weave, yet they are clothed; They neither sow nor reap, yet they have food. There are birds of song and dance; the luan bird sings by itself, while the feng bird dances on its own. There are a hundred beasts living together in groups here. It is where the hundred grains gather.
