
1  海内西经:
海内西南陬以北者。
The southernmost regions of the realm to the northward.
2  海内西经:
贰负之臣曰危，危与贰负杀窫窳。帝乃梏之䟽属之山，桎其右足，反缚两手与发，系之山上木。在开题西北。
Erfu's minister was named Wei, and Wei together with Erfu killed Yayu. The emperor then bound him in the Shushu Mountain, shackled his right foot, tied both his hands and hair behind his back, and fastened them to a tree on the mountain. It is located northwest of Kaiqi.
3  海内西经:
大泽方百里，群鸟所生及所解。在雁门北。
A large marsh measuring a hundred li in area, where flocks of birds are born and molt their feathers. It is located north of Yanmen.
4  海内西经:
雁门山，雁出其间，在高柳北。
Yanmen Mountain, through which wild geese pass, lies to the north of Gaoliu.
5  海内西经:
高柳在代北。
Gaoliu is located to the north of Dai.
6  海内西经:
后稷之葬，山水环之，在氐国西。
The burial site of Houji, encircled by mountains and rivers, lies west of the Di Kingdom.
7  海内西经:
流黄酆氏之国，中方三百里。有涂四方，中有山。在后稷葬西。
The country of Liuhuang Fengshi has a central area measuring three hundred li in size. It has roads extending in four directions, with a mountain at its center. It is located west of Houji's burial site.
8  海内西经:
流沙出锺山，西行又南行昆仑之墟，西南入海黑水之山。
lxml The Flowing Sands originate from Zhongshan, flow westward and then southward to the ruins of Kunlun, and finally enter the western part of the Black Water Mountains into the sea.
9  海内西经:
东胡在大泽东。
The Donghu are located east of the large marsh.
10  海内西经:
夷人在东胡东。
The Yiren people are located to the east of the Donghu.
11  海内西经:
貊国在汉水东北。地近于燕，灭之。
The Mo Kingdom is located northeast of the Hanshui River. Its territory was close to Yan, which later conquered it.
12  海内西经:
孟鸟在貊国东北，其鸟文赤、黄、青、东乡。
Mengniao is located northeast of the Mo Kingdom; its bird has patterns in red, yellow, and green, facing east.
13  海内西经:
海内昆仑之墟，在西北，帝之下都。昆仑之墟，方八百里，高万仞。上有木禾，长五寻，大五围。面有九井，以玉为槛。面有九门，门有开明兽守之，百神之所在。在八隅之岩，赤水之际，非仁羿莫能上冈之岩。
Within the realm, the ruins of Kunlun are located in the northwest, serving as the lower capital of the emperor. The ruins of Kunlun measure eight hundred li on each side and rise ten thousand ren in height. There are grain-bearing trees on top, growing five xun tall with trunks as thick as five wei. Each face has nine wells, with railings made of jade. Each face has nine gates, and at each gate there is a Kaoming beast guarding it; this is where the hundred deities reside. It lies in the cliffs of the eight corners, at the banks of the Cishui River; only the benevolent Yi could ascend the rocky ridge.
14  海内西经:
赤水出东南隅，以行其东北。西南流注南海厌火东。
The Cishui River originates from the southeast corner and flows toward its northeast. It flows southwestward, emptying into the South Sea east of Yanhuo.
15  海内西经:
河水出东北隅，以行其北，西南又入渤海，又出海外，即西而北，入禹所导积石山。
The Yellow River originates from the northeast corner, flows northward, then turns southwest to enter the Bohai Sea. It emerges again beyond the sea, flowing west and then north into the Jishan Mountains guided by Yu.
16  海内西经:
洋水、黑水出西北隅，以东，东行，又东北，南入海，羽民南。
The Yangshui and Heishui rivers originate from the northwest corner, flow eastward, then northeastward, and finally turn south to enter the sea at the southern part of Yumin.
17  海内西经:
弱水、青水出西南隅，以东，又北，又西南，过毕方鸟东。
The Ruoshui and Qingshui rivers originate from the southwest corner, flow eastward, turn northward, then southwestward again, passing to the east of the Bifang bird.
18  海内西经:
昆仑南渊深三百仞。开明兽身大类虎而九首，皆人面，东向立昆仑上。
The southern abyss of Kunlun is three hundred ren deep. The Kaoming beast has a body similar to that of a tiger but with nine heads, each having a human face; they stand facing east on Kunlun.
19  海内西经:
开明西有凤凰、鸾鸟，皆戴蛇践蛇，膺有赤蛇。
To the west of Kaoming are phoenixes and luan birds, all wearing snakes on their heads and treading upon snakes; their breasts bear red snakes.
20  海内西经:
开明北有视肉、珠树、文玉树、玗琪树、不死树。凤凰、鸾鸟皆载瞂。又有离朱、木禾、柏树、甘水、圣木、曼兑，一曰挺木牙交。
To the north of Kaoming are Shirou, pearl trees, patterned jade trees, Yuqi trees, and immortality trees. Both phoenixes and luan birds ride on clouds. There are also Lizhu, grain-bearing trees, cypress trees, Ganshui, sacred trees, Mandui; another name for it is Tingmu Yajiao.
21  海内西经:
开明东有巫彭、巫抵、巫阳、巫履、巫凡、巫相，夹窫窳之尸，皆操不死之药以距之。窫窳者，蛇身人面，贰负臣所杀也。
To the east of Kaoming are Wupeng, Wudi, Wuyang, Wulv, Wufan, and Wuxiang; they stand on both sides of Yayu's corpse, each holding immortality elixirs to ward it off. Yayu had a serpent body and human face; he was killed by Erfu's minister.
22  海内西经:
服常树，其上有三头人，伺琅玕树。
lxml The Fu Changshu tree has three-headed people on it, watching over the langgan trees.
23  海内西经:
开明南有树鸟，六首；蛟、蝮、蛇、蜼、豹、鸟秩树，于表池树木，诵鸟、鶽、视肉。
To the south of Kaoming is a Shu bird with six heads; There are jiao, fang snakes, weizi, leopards, and bird-zhi trees; outside the pool's trees are songniao, yu birds, and Shirou.
