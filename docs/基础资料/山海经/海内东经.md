
1  海内东经:
海内东北陬以南者。
The easternmost regions of the realm to the southward.
2  海内东经:
钜燕在东北陬。
Juyan is located in the northeastern corner.
3  海内东经:
国在流沙中者埻端、玺㬇，在昆仑墟东南。一曰海内之郡，不为郡县，在流沙中。
The states of Chuduan and Xixia, which are located within the Flowing Sands, lie to the southeast of Kunlun Xu. One account says that the commanderies within the realm do not establish commanderies or counties and are located within the Flowing Sands.
4  海内东经:
国在流沙外者，大夏、竖沙、居繇、月支之国。
The states that lie outside the Flowing Sands include Daxia, Shusha, Juyao, and the state of Yuezhi.
5  海内东经:
西胡白玉山，在大夏东，苍梧在白玉山西南，皆在流沙西，昆仑墟东南。昆仑山在西胡西，皆在西北。
The White Jade Mountain of Xihu lies to the east of Daxia, and Cangwu is located to the southwest of the White Jade Mountain. Both are situated west of the Flowing Sands and southeast of Kunlun Xu. The Kunlun Mountains lie to the west of Xihu, and all are located in the northwest.
6  海内东经:
雷泽中有雷神，龙身而人头，鼓其腹。在吴西。
In Leize there is a thunder god, with a dragon's body and a human head, who beats his belly to produce thunder. It lies to the west of Wu.
7  海内东经:
都州在海中。一曰郁州。
Duzhou is located in the sea. One account says Yuzhou.
8  海内东经:
琅瑘台在渤海间，琅瑘之东。其北有山。一曰在海间。
Langtai is located between Bohai and to the east of Langshan. To its north there are mountains. One account says it is located between the seas.
9  海内东经:
韩雁在海中，都州南。
Hanyan lies in the sea, to the south of Duzhou.
10  海内东经:
始鸠在海中，辕厉南。
Shijiu is located in the sea, to the south of Yuanli.
11  海内东经:
会稽山在大楚南。
The Kuaiji Mountains lie to the south of Da Chu.
12  海内东经:
岷三江：首…大江出汶山，北江出曼山，南江出高山。高山在城都西。入海，在长州南。浙江出三天子都，在其东。在闽西北，入海，馀暨南。庐江出三天子都，入江，彭泽西。一曰天子鄣。淮水出馀山，馀山在朝阳东，义乡西，入海，淮浦北。湘水出舜葬东南陬，西环之。入洞庭下。一曰东南西泽。汉水出鲋鱼之山，帝颛顼葬于阳，九嫔葬于阴，四蛇卫之。蒙水出汉阳西，入江，聂阳西。温水出崆峒，崆峒山在临汾南，入河，华阳北。颍水出少室，少室山在雍氏南，入淮西鄢北。一曰缑氏。汝水出天息山，在梁勉乡西南，入淮极西北。一曰淮在期思北。泾水出长城北山，山在郁郅长垣北，北入渭，戏北。渭水出鸟鼠同穴山，东注河，入华阴北。白水出蜀，而东南注江，入江州城下。沅水山出象郡镡城西，入东注江，入下隽西，合洞庭中。赣水出聂都东山，东北注江，入彭泽西。泗水出吴东北而南，西南过湖陵西，而东南注东海，入淮阴北。郁水出象郡，而西南注南海，入须陵东南。肄水出临晋西南，而东南注海，入番禺西。潢水出桂阳西北山，东南注肄水，入敦浦西。洛水出洛西山，东北注河，入成皋之西。汾水出上窳北，而西南注河，入皮氏南。沁水出井陉山东，东南注河，入怀东南。济水出共山南东丘，绝钜鹿泽，注渤海，入齐琅槐东北。潦水出卫皋东，东南注渤海，入潦阳。宓沱水出晋阳城南，而西至阳曲北，而东注渤海，入越章武北。漳水出山阳东，东注渤海，入章武南。
Three rivers originate from Min: The first...the Great River originates from Wenshan, the Northern River from Manshan, and the Southern River from Gaoshan. Gaoshan is located to the west of Chengdu. They flow into the sea, south of Zhangzhou. The Zhejiang River originates from Sandaizidu and flows to its east. It lies in the northwest of Min, flowing into the sea south of Yuji. The Lujiang River originates from Sandaizudou, flows into the Yangtze River, and is located west of Pengze. One account says Tianzizhang. The Huai River originates from Yushan, which lies to the east of Chaoyang and west of Yixiang. The river flows into the sea north of Huaipu. The Xiang River originates from the southeastern corner of Shun's burial site and circles around it to the west. It flows into Lake Dongting below. One account says "Dongnanxizhe." The Hanshui River originates from Fuyu Mountain. Emperor Zhuanxu was buried on its southern side, while his nine consorts were buried on the northern side, guarded by four serpents. The Mengshui River originates to the west of Hanyang and flows into the Yangtze to the west of Nieyang. The Wenshui River originates from Kongsong, and Kongsong Mountain lies to the south of Linfen. The river flows into the Yellow River north of Huayang. The Yingshui River originates from Shaoshi Mountain, which is located to the south of Yongshi. The river flows into the western part of the Huai River north of Yan. One account says Goushi. The Rushui River originates from Tianxi Mountain, which is located southwest of Liangmian Xiang, and flows into the extreme northwest of the Huai. One account says the Huai is north of Qisi. The Jing River originates from a mountain to the north of the Great Wall, which lies north of Yuzhi Changyuan. The river flows north into the Wei River, to the north of Xi. The Weishui River originates from Niaoshutongxue Mountain and flows eastward into the Yellow River, entering north of Huayin. The Baishui River originates from Shu and flows southeast into the Yangtze, entering beneath Jiangzhou City. The Yuan River originates from a mountain west of Dancheng in Xiang Jun, flows east into the Yangtze and enters to the west of Xiajun, converging in the middle of Lake Dongting. The Gan River originates from Mount Dongshan in Niedu and flows northeast into the Yangtze, emptying to the west of Pengze. The Si River originates northeast of Wu and flows south, passing west of Luling in the southwest direction, then turns southeast to flow into the East Sea, entering north of Huaiyin. The Yu River originates from Xiang Jun and flows southwest into the South China Sea, emptying southeast of Xuling. The Yishui River originates in the southwest of Linjin and flows southeast into the sea, entering to the west of Panyu. The Huangshui River originates from a mountain in the northwest of Guiyang and flows southeast into Yishui, entering to the west of Dunpu. The Luo River originates from Luoxi Mountain and flows northeast into the Yellow River, emptying at its western side near Chenggao. The Fen River originates north of Shangyu and flows southwest into the Yellow River, entering south of Pishi. The Qinshui River originates east of Jingxing Mountain and flows southeast into the Yellow River, emptying in the southeast of Huai. The Ji River originates from the southern eastern hill of Gong Mountain, flows through Ju Lu Lake, and empties into Bohai Sea northeast of Langhuai in Qi. The Lao River originates east of Weigao, flows southeast into the Bohai Sea, and enters Laoyang. The Hutuo River originates south of Jinyang City, flows west to the north of Yangqu, then turns east into Bohai Sea and enters Beizhangwu in Yue. The Zhang River originates east of Shanyang, flows east into the Bohai Sea, entering to the south of Zhangwu.
13  海内东经:
建平元年四月丙戌，待诏太常属臣望校治，侍中光禄勋臣龚、侍中奉车都尉光禄大夫臣秀领主省。
On the Bingxu day of the fourth month (May 13, 48 AD), Daizhao Taichang Shuchen Wang examined and compiled it. It was supervised by Shizhong Guangluxun Chenguang and Shizhong Fengche Duyi Guanglvdafu Chenshow.
