
1  北山经:
《北山经》之首，曰单狐之山，多机木，其上多华草。漨水出焉，而西流注于泑水，其中多芘石文石。
The first mountain of the Beishan Jing is called Danhu Sh 山, which has many ji mu trees and on its slopes there are many hua cao plants. The Chong Shui springs from here, flowing westward to join the You Shui; there are many bi shi and wen shi stones in it.
2  北山经:
又北二百五十里，曰求如之山，其上多铜，其下多玉，无草木。滑水出焉，而西流注于诸毗之水。其中多滑鱼，其状如鱓，赤背，其音如梧，食之已疣。其中多水马，其状如马，文臂牛尾，其音如呼。
Another two hundred and fifty li to the north is Qiuru Sh 山; its upper slopes have much copper, while its lower slopes have much jade, with no grass or trees. The Hua Shui springs from there and flows westward into the Zhubi Shui. There are many hua yu fish in it; they resemble shuan, have red backs, and their sound is like that of wu. Eating them cures warts. There are also many shui ma in it; they look like horses, have patterned forearms and ox tails, and their sound is similar to a shout.
3  北山经:
又北三百里，曰带山，其上多玉，其下多青碧。有兽焉，其状如马，一角有错，其名曰䑏䟽，可以辟火。有鸟焉，其状如乌，五彩而赤文，名曰鵸鵌，是自为牝牡，食之不疽。彭水出焉，而西流注于芘湖之水，其中多儵鱼，其状如鷄而赤毛，三尾、六足、四首，其音如鹊，食之可以已忧。
Another three hundred li to the north lies Dai Sh 山; it has much jade on its upper slopes and much qing bi below. There is a beast there; it resembles a horse, has one horn with scales, and its name is Kuishu. It can be used to ward off fire. There is a bird there; it resembles the wu, has five colors and red patterns, and its name is Yujiu. It naturally possesses both male and female characteristics. Eating it prevents boils. The Peng Shui springs from there, flowing westward into the Bihu Shui. There are many shu yu fish in it; these resemble chickens with red feathers, have three tails, six feet, four heads, and their sound resembles that of a magpie. Eating them can relieve worries.
4  北山经:
又北四百里，曰谯明之山，谯水出焉，西流注于河。其中多何罗之鱼，一首而十身，其音如吠犬，食之已痈。有兽焉，其状如貆而赤豪，其音如榴榴，名曰孟槐，可以御凶。是山也，无草木，多青雄黄。
Another four hundred li to the north is Jiaoming Sh 山; the Jiao Shui springs from there, and flows westward into the Yellow River. There are many Heluo yu fish in it, with one head and ten bodies; their sound is like a barking dog. Eating them can cure abscesses. There is also a beast there; its appearance resembles that of the huan but with red fur, and its sound is like "liuliu." Its name is Menghuai. It can be used to repel evil. This mountain has no grass or trees, but contains much green cinnabar and realgar.
5  北山经:
又北三百五十里，曰涿光之山，嚣水出焉，而西流注于河。其中多鳛鳛之鱼，其状如鹊而十翼，鳞皆在羽端，其音如鹊，可以御火，食之不瘅。其上多松柏，其下多椶橿，其兽多麢羊，其鸟多蕃。
Another three hundred and fifty li to the north lies Zhuoguang Sh 山; from there springs the Xiao Shui, which flows westward into the river. There are many Xixi yu fish in it. They resemble magpies but have ten wings, with scales all located at the tips of their feathers. Their sound is like that of a magpie; they can be used to ward off fires and eating them prevents boils. Its upper slopes have many pine and cypress trees, while its lower areas are abundant in zong jia. The beasts there include many ling yang, and the birds are mostly fan.
6  北山经:
又北三百八十里，曰虢山，其上多漆，其下多桐椐，其阳多玉，其阴多铁。伊水出焉，西流注于河。其兽多橐驼，其鸟多寓，状如鼠而鸟翼，其音如羊，可以御兵。
Another three hundred and eighty li to the north is Guo Sh 山. Its upper slopes have much lacquer, while its lower parts are rich in tong and ju trees. The southern side has much jade, and the northern side has much iron. The Yi Shui springs from there, flows westward into the Yellow River (Huang He). The beasts there are mostly tuo tuo camels, and the birds include many yu. The yu resemble mice with bird wings; their sound is like that of a sheep, and they can be used to repel war.
7  北山经:
又北四百里，至于虢山之尾，其上多玉而无石。鱼水出焉，而西流注于河，其中多文贝。
Another four hundred li north brings one to the tail end of Guo Sh 山; there is much jade on it, but no stone. The Yu Shui springs from here and flows westward into the River; there are many patterned shells in it.
8  北山经:
又北二百里，曰丹熏之山，其上多樗柏，其草多韭薤，多丹雘。熏水出焉，而西流注于棠水。有兽焉，其状如鼠，而菟首麋身，其音如獋犬，以其尾飞，名曰耳鼠，食之不䐆，又可以御百毒。
Another two hundred li to the north is Danxun Sh 山; its slopes have many chu bai trees, and among its grasses there are much leeks and xie. It also has much dan huo. The Xun Shui springs from here and flows southwestward into the Tang Shui. There is a beast there, which resembles a rat with the head of a tu and the body of a mi. Its sound is like that of an old dog; it flies using its tail. It is called Ershu. Eating it prevents abdominal pain, and it can also be used to guard against all poisons.
9  北山经:
又北二百八十里，曰石者之山，其上无草木，多瑶碧。泚水出焉，而西流注于河，有兽焉，其状如豹，而文题白身，名曰孟极，是善伏，其鸣自呼。
Another two hundred and eighty li north is Shizhe Sh 山; there are no grasses or trees on its slopes, but it has much yao bi. The Ci Shui springs from there and flows southwestward into the Yellow River; there is a beast here, which resembles a leopard with patterned head and white body. Its name is Mengji. It is good at hiding, and its call sounds like its own name.
10  北山经:
又北百一十里，曰边春之山，多葱、葵、韭、桃、李。杠水出焉，而西流注于泑泽。有兽焉，其状如禺而文身，善笑，见人则卧，名曰幽鴳，其鸣自呼。
Another one hundred and ten li to the north lies Bianchun Sh 山, where there are many leeks, kui, leeks again, peaches, and plums. The Gang Shui springs from here and flows southward into You Ze. There is a beast here; it resembles yu and has patterned body, loves to laugh, lies down when seeing people. Its name is Youan, its call sounds like its own name (Youan).
11  北山经:
又北二百里，曰蔓联之山，其上无草木。有兽焉，其状如禺而有鬣，牛尾、文臂、马蹄，见人则呼，名曰足訾，其鸣自呼。有鸟焉，群居而朋飞，其毛如雌雉，名曰鵁，其名自呼，食之已风。
Another two hundred li north is Manlian Sh 山; there are no trees or grasses on it. There is a beast here, resembling yu with a mane, ox tail, patterned arms and horse hooves. It shouts when seeing people; its name is Zuzi, whose call sounds like itself (Zuzi). There is a bird here, living in flocks and flying together; its feathers resemble those of the female pheasant. Its name is Xie, which calls out its own name (Xie). Eating it can cure wind-related ailments.
12  北山经:
又北百八十里，曰单张之山，其上无草木。有兽焉，其状如豹而长尾，人首而牛耳，一目，名曰诸犍，善吒，行则衘其尾，居则蟠其尾。有鸟焉，其状如雉，而文首、白翼、黄足，名曰白鵺，食之已嗌痛，可以已痸。栎水出焉，而南流注于杠水。
Another one hundred and eighty li north lies Danzhang Sh 山, on which there are no trees or grass. There is a beast here. It resembles a leopard with a long tail, has a human head and ox ears, one eye; its name is Zhuqian. It makes loud noises, carries its tail in the mouth when moving, and coils it up when resting. There is a bird here; it resembles the pheasant, with patterned head, white wings and yellow feet. Its name is Baiji. Eating it cures sore throat, can also cure convulsions. The Li Shui springs from here and flows southerly into the Gang Shui.
13  北山经:
又北三百二十里，曰灌题之山，其上多樗柘，其下多流沙，多砥。有兽焉，其状如牛而白尾，其音如訆，名曰那父。有鸟焉，其状如雌雉而人面，见人则跃，名曰竦斯，其鸣自呼也。匠韩之水出焉，而西流注于泑泽，其中多磁石。
Another three hundred and twenty li north is Guanti Sh 山; its upper parts have many chu zhe trees, while the lower areas are full of flowing sand and contain much di. There is a beast here; its shape resembles that of an ox with a white tail, and its sound like "zhi." Its name is Nafu. There is a bird here; its shape resembles the female pheasant with a human face, and it jumps when seeing people. Its name is Songsi, whose call sounds like itself. The Jianghan Shui springs from here and flows westerly into You Ze; there are many lodestones in it.
14  北山经:
又北二百里，曰潘侯之山，其上多松柏，其下多榛楛，其阳多玉，其阴多铁。有兽焉，其状如牛，而四节生毛，名曰旄牛。边水出焉，而南流注于栎泽。
Another two hundred li north lies Panhou Sh 山; its upper areas have many pine and cypress, while the lower parts are abundant in hazel and kuhu. Its southern side has much jade and northern side contains much iron. There is a beast here; resembling an ox, with fur growing on its four joints. Its name is Maoniu. The Bian Shui springs from there and flows southward into Li Ze.
15  北山经:
又北二百三十里，曰小咸之山，无草木，冬夏有雪。
Another two hundred and thirty li north is Xiaoxian Sh 山, with no trees or grass; it has snow in winter and summer.
16  北山经:
北二百八十里，曰大咸之山，无草木，其下多玉。是山也，四方，不可以上。有蛇名曰长蛇，其毛如彘豪，其音如鼓柝。
Two hundred and eighty li north is Daxian Sh 山, without trees or grass; its lower parts have much jade. This mountain is square in shape and cannot be climbed to the top. There is a snake here named Changshe, with fur like that of a pig; its sound resembles the clacking of a night watchman's stick.
17  北山经:
又北三百二十里，曰敦薨之山，其上多椶楠，其下多茈草。敦薨之水出焉，而西流注于泑泽。出于昆仑之东北隅，实惟河源。其中多赤鲑，其兽多兕、旄牛，其鸟多鳲鸠。
Another three hundred and twenty li to the north is Dunhong Sh 山; its upper part has many zong nan trees, while its lower area abounds in zi cao. The Dunhong Shui springs from there and flows westerly to join You Ze. It originates from the northeast corner of Kunlun, which is indeed the source of the Yellow River (He Yuan). There are many red salmon in it; among its beasts there are many si and mao niu, while the birds include mostly shi jiu.
18  北山经:
又北二百里，曰少咸之山，无草木，多青碧。有兽焉，其状如牛，而赤身、人面、马足，名曰窫窳，其音如婴儿，是食人。敦水出焉，东流注于雁门之水，其中多䰽䰽之鱼，食之杀人。
Another two hundred li to the north lies Shaoxian Sh 山; it has no grass or trees and contains much qing bi. There is a beast here; shaped like an ox, with red body, human face and horse feet. Its name is Yayu, whose sound resembles that of an infant; it preys on humans. The Dun Shui springs from here and flows eastward into Yanmen Shui; there are many Huanhuan yu fish in it, eating which can kill a person.
19  北山经:
又北二百里，曰狱法之山，瀤泽之水出焉，而东北流注于泰泽。其中多䲃鱼，其状如鲤而鸡足，食之已疣。有兽焉，其状如犬而人面，善投，见人则笑，其名山𤟤，其行如风，见则天下大风。
Another two hundred li to the north lie Yufa Sh 山; from here springs the Tu Ze Shui, flowing northeastward into Taize. There are many Ji Yu fish in it; they resemble carp but have chicken feet. Eating them cures warts (you). There is a beast here; in shape it resembles a dog with a human face. It excels at throwing, and smiles when seeing people. Its name Shangshan, moves as fast as the wind; its appearance foretells great winds across the world.
20  北山经:
又北一百里，曰北岳之山，多枳棘刚木。有兽焉，其状如牛而四角，人目、彘耳，其名曰诸怀，其音如鸣雁，是食人。诸怀之水出焉，而西流注于嚣水，其中多鮨鱼，鱼身而犬首，其音如婴儿，食之已狂。
Another one hundred li to the north lies Beiyue Sh 山, abundant in zhi ji and gang mu trees. There is a beast here; resembles an ox with four horns, human eyes and pig ears. Its name is Zhuhuai, whose sound like that of a geese call; it preys on people. The Zhuhuai Shui springs from here and flows to the west into Xiao Shui; there are many Yuyu fish in it, which have fish bodies but dog heads. Their sound resembles that of an infant. Eating them cures madness (kuang).
21  北山经:
又北百八十里，曰浑夕之山，无草木，多铜玉。嚣水出焉，而西北流注于海。有蛇一首两身，名曰肥遗，见则其国大旱。
Another one hundred and eighty li to the north lies Hunxi Sh 山, devoid of grass or trees but rich in copper and jade. The Xiao Shui springs from here and flowing northwestward into the sea. There is a snake with one head but two bodies, named Feiyi; its appearance heralds great drought in that country.
22  北山经:
又北五十里，曰北单之山，无草木，多葱韭。
Another fifty li to the north lies Beidan Sh 山, lacking trees or grass but abundant in leeks and chives (jiu).
23  北山经:
又北百里，曰罴差之山，无草木，多马。
Another one hundred li north is Beicha Sh 山, barren of trees or grass but rich in horses.
24  北山经:
又北百八十里，曰北鲜之山，是多马。鲜水出焉，而西北流注于涂吾之水。
Another one hundred and eighty li further north lies Beixian Sh 山; this place is abundant with horses. The Xian Shui springs from here, flows northwestward into Tuwu Shui.
25  北山经:
又北百七十里，曰堤山，多马。有兽焉，其状如豹而文首，名曰狕。堤水出焉，而东流注于泰泽，其中多龙龟。
Another one hundred and seventy li to the north lies Di Sh 山; it is rich in horses. There is a beast here, shaped like a leopard with patterned head; its name is Huo. The Di Shui springs from there and flowing eastward into Tai Ze, where there are many long gui turtles in it.
26  北山经:
凡《北山经》之首，自单狐之山至于堤山，凡二十五山，五千四百九十里，其神皆人面蛇身。其祠之，毛用一雄鷄彘瘗，吉玉用一圭，瘗而不糈。其山北人，皆生食不火之物。
The first part of the Beishan Jing begins with Danhu Sh 山 and ends at Di Sh 山, totaling twenty-five mountains covering five thousand four hundred and ninety li. The deities associated with these mountains all have human faces and snake bodies. To worship them, one should offer a single male chicken or pig as sacrifice, buried in the ground. A single gui-shaped jade is used for offerings; it should be buried without any sacrificial millet (xu). The people living to the north of these mountains all eat raw food, uncooked by fire.
27  北山经:
北次二经之首，在河之东，其首枕汾，其名曰管涔之山。其上无木而多草，其下多玉。汾水出焉，而西流注于河。
The first mountain in the second northern section lies to the east of the Yellow River, with its head resting on Fen He. Its name is Guancen Sh 山. There are no trees on it but many grasses; below there is much jade. The Fen Shui springs from here and flows in a westerly direction to join the Yellow River (Huanghe).
28  北山经:
又西二百五十里，曰少阳之山，其上多玉，其下多赤银。酸水出焉，而东流注于汾水，其中多美赭。
Another two hundred and fifty li west is Shaoyang Sh 山; its upper reaches have much jade, while the lower areas contain much red silver. The Suan Shui springs from here and flows easterly into Fen Shui; it contains many fine cinnamons (mei zhe).
29  北山经:
又北五十里，曰县雍之山，其上多玉，其下多铜，其兽多闾麋，其鸟多白翟白䳑，晋水出焉，而东南流注于汾水。其中多鮆鱼，其状如鯈而赤鳞，其音如叱，食之不骄。
Another fifty li north is Xuanyong Sh 山; its upper area has much jade, while the lower part contains much copper. The beasts here include many lu mi, and the birds mostly white zhi and bai ji. The Jin Shui springs from there flows southeastward into Fen Shui. There are many Jiyu fish in it; they resemble tiao but have red scales, their sound like "chi," and eating them prevents arrogance.
30  北山经:
又北二百里，曰狐岐之山，无草木，多青碧。胜水出焉，而东北流注于汾水，其中多苍玉。
Another two hundred li north is Huqi Sh 山; it lacks grass or trees, but has much qing bi (green jade). The Sheng Shui springs from here and flows northeastward into Fen Shui, where there is much cangyu (blue jade).
31  北山经:
又北三百五十里，曰白沙山，广员三百里，尽沙也，无草木鸟兽。鲔水出于其上，潜于其下，是多白玉。
Another three hundred and fifty li north lies Baisha Sh 山, a circular area of three hundred li in diameter, entirely covered with sand; there are no grass, trees, birds or beasts. The Yu Shui originates from the upper part of this mountain and flows underground beneath it; there is much white jade here.
32  北山经:
又北四百里，曰尔是之山，无草木，无水。
Another four hundred li north lies Ershi Sh 山, with neither grass nor trees and no water.
33  北山经:
又北三百八十里，曰狂山，无草木。是山也，冬夏有雪。狂水出焉，而西流注于浮水，其中多美玉。
Another three hundred and eighty li north is Kuang Sh 山, also devoid of grass or trees. This mountain has snow in both winter and summer. The Kuang Shui springs from here, and flows westward to join Fu Shui; there are many fine jades in it.
34  北山经:
又北三百八十里，曰诸馀之山，其上多铜玉，其下多松柏。诸馀之水出焉，而东流注于旄水。
Another three hundred and eighty li further north is Zhuyu Sh 山; its upper regions have much copper and jade, while the lower slopes are covered with many pine and cypress trees. The Zhuyu Shui springs from here and flows southeastward to join Mao Shui.
35  北山经:
又北三百五十里，曰敦头之山，其上多金玉，无草木。旄水出焉，而东流注于印泽，其中多𩣡马，牛尾而白身，一角其音如呼。
Another three hundred and fifty li further north is Duntuo Sh 山; its upper region has much gold and jade, but no grass or trees. The Mao Shui springs from here and flows toward the east into Yin Ze; there are many Huangma in it, which resemble oxen with white bodies, have one horn, and make a sound like a shout.
36  北山经:
又北三百五十里，曰钩吾之山，其上多玉，其下多铜。有兽焉，其状如羊身人面，其目在腋下，虎齿人爪，其音如婴儿，名曰狍鴞，是食人。
Another three hundred and fifty li farther north is Gouwu Sh 山; its upper slope has much jade, while the lower area contains much copper. There is a beast here; with the body of a sheep and human face, its eyes are located under the armpits, it has tiger teeth and human claws. Its sound resembles that of an infant, named Paoxiao, which preys on humans.
37  北山经:
又北三百里，曰北嚣之山，无石，其阳多碧，其阴多玉。有兽焉，其状如虎，而白身犬首，马尾彘鬣，名曰独𤞞。有鸟焉，其状如乌，人面，名曰𪄀𪃑，宵飞而昼伏，食之已暍。涔水出焉，而东流注于卭泽。
Another three hundred li north is Beixiao Sh 山; there are few stones here, with much bi (a kind of jade) on its southern side and much jade on the northern slope. There is a beast here; which resembles a tiger, with white body and dog head, horse tail and pig bristles. Its name is Dubi. There is a bird here; which resembles the wu with human face. Its name is Huihui, it flies at night and hides during the day. Eating it can cure heatstroke (ye). The Cen Shui springs from here flows eastward into Qiong Ze.
38  北山经:
又北三百五十里，曰梁渠之山，无草木，多金玉。修水出焉，而东流注于雁门，其兽多居暨，其状如汇而赤毛，其音如豚，有鸟焉，其状如夸父，四翼、一目、犬尾，名曰嚣，其音如鹊，食之已腹痛，可以止同。
Another three hundred and fifty li north is Liangqu Sh 山; it has neither grass nor trees, but contains much gold and jade. The Xiu Shui springs from here, flowing eastward to join Yanmen. The beasts there are mostly Juji; they resemble Hui but have red fur and make a sound like that of a piglet. There is also a bird resembling Kuafu with four wings, one eye, and a dog's tail. Its name is Xiao, which makes a sound similar to that of a magpie. Eating it can relieve abdominal pain and stop diarrhea.
39  北山经:
又北四百里，曰姑灌之山，无草木，是山也，冬夏有雪。
Another four hundred li north is Guguan Sh 山; there are no grass or trees here, and snow falls on this mountain in both winter and summer.
40  北山经:
又北三百八十里，曰湖灌之山，其阳多玉，其阴多碧，多马。湖灌之水出焉，而东流注于海，其中多䱇。有木焉，其叶如柳而赤理。
Another three hundred and eighty li north is Huguan Sh 山; the southern side has much jade, while its northern slope contains much bi (a kind of green stone), with many horses. The Huguan Shui springs from here, flows eastward to join the sea; there are many Xian fish in it. There is a kind of tree here, whose leaves resemble those of willow with red patterns.
41  北山经:
又北水行五百里，流沙三百里，至于洹山，其上多金玉。三桑生之，其树皆无枝，其高百仞。百果树生之，其下多怪蛇。

43  北山经:

The first part of the "Bei Ci Er Jing" begins from Guancen Sh 山 to Duntu Sh 山, totaling seventeen mountains and covering a distance of five thousand six hundred and ninety li. Their deities all have the bodies of snakes and human faces. The ritual for worship: offer one male chicken and a pig, buried in the ground; use one bi (a flat jade disk) and one gui (an elongated jade), cast them into water without offering millet.
44  北山经:

There is a bird there; which resembles the magpie, with white body, red tail and six legs. Its name is Xie, it is easily startled, and its call announces itself.

There is a beast here which resembles a rabbit with the head of a mouse and flies on its back; it is called Feishu. The Mian Shui springs from here but flows underground, with much Huange in it.

There is a bird here which resembles the female pheasant, with five colors and patterns. It can determine its own gender; it is named Xiangshe, whose call announces itself. The Liu Shui springs from here then flows southward to join the Yellow River (He). There are Fufu fish in it, which resemble Fu Yu, with the head of a fish and body of a pig. The first of all mountains in the Bei Ci San Jing is Taixing Sh 山, extending to Wufeng Sh 山. In total there are forty-six mountains covering twelve thousand three hundred and fifty li. Twenty of the deities have the form of a horse's body with human faces. When offering sacrifices to them, one should use one zaochai and bury it. Fourteen of the deities have forms like pigs carrying jade. When offering sacrifices to them, use jade and do not bury it. Ten of the deities have pig-like bodies with eight legs and snake tails. When offering sacrifices to them, offer one bi disc and bury it. In total there are forty-four deities; all of them should be worshipped with tu rice grains, and none of them consume fire or cooked food.
