# Realmforge (铸造世界) — World & Lore Design Document

> A children's crafting/forging game set in the mythical world of 《山海经》.
> Players explore, discover, and shape the world — even bringing pieces of the real world into it.

## 1. The Two Factions

In the world of Shanhai, two great tribes emerged from the first forge — the primordial fire that cracked open the egg of chaos. They share the same origin but diverged in philosophy. They are not enemies in the traditional sense; rather, they are **rival siblings** who believe the world needs different things to thrive.

### 1.1 焱族 — The Flame Tribe (Yan)

**Philosophy: "To shape is to change."**

The Yan tribe believes that the world is raw material waiting to be transformed. They see beauty in change, growth, and innovation. Their motto is **"破而后立"** — break the old to build the new.

**Core Values:**
- **Courage** — dare to try, dare to fail, dare to try again
- **Creativity** — every problem has a wild new solution
- **Expression** — make your mark on the world

**Visual Aesthetic:**
- Warm color palette: reds, oranges, golds, and deep blacks
- Jagged, asymmetrical designs that look like flames or flowing lava
- Clothing adorned with feather-like patterns (inspired by the 凤凰/vermilion bird)
- Their forges are open-air volcanic pits where magma bubbles and sparks fly
- Architecture: tall spires, curved roofs that sweep upward like rising flames

**Approach to Crafting:**
- Fast, bold, experimental. They throw things into the forge and see what happens.
- They love combining wildly different materials — "What if we mix thunder-stone with butterfly wing?"
- Their creations tend to be powerful but unpredictable.
- Signature technique: **"熔变" (Melt-Shift)** — heating materials until they transform into something entirely new.

**Notable Characters:**
- **焱奶奶 (Grandma Yan)** — the elder who speaks in riddles and laughs when things explode. She teaches that "every mistake is just a surprise you didn't expect yet."
- **小烈 (Little Lie/Fierce)** — a young apprentice who is enthusiastic but impatient. Kids relate to their eagerness to skip steps.

### 1.2 渊族 — The Abyss Tribe (Yuan)

**Philosophy: "To shape is to listen."**

The Yuan tribe believes the world already holds all the answers — you just need to listen carefully enough to hear them. Their motto is **"顺其自然"** — follow the natural way.

**Core Values:**
- **Patience** — good things come to those who wait and watch
- **Harmony** — work with nature, not against it
- **Wisdom** — understand before you act

**Visual Aesthetic:**
- Cool color palette: deep blues, teals, jade greens, silver, and moon-white
- Flowing, symmetrical designs inspired by water ripples and ocean currents
- Clothing adorned with scale-like patterns and shell ornaments (inspired by sea creatures and the 鲲/leviathan)
- Their forges are underwater crystal chambers where geothermal vents glow softly through translucent stone
- Architecture: rounded, organic structures built into cliffs and coral reefs, glowing with bioluminescence

**Approach to Crafting:**
- Slow, deliberate, precise. They study a material for a long time before touching it.
- They believe each material has a "spirit shape" inside it — the crafter's job is to reveal it.
- Their creations tend to be elegant, stable, and long-lasting.
- Signature technique: **"渊铸" (Abyss Forge)** — using water pressure and crystal resonance to shape materials gently over time.

**Notable Characters:**
- **渊爷爷 (Grandpa Yuan)** — speaks slowly, tells long stories, always has a cup of tea. He teaches by asking questions rather than giving answers.
- **小澜 (Little Lan/Wave)** — a curious young apprentice who loves collecting and cataloging materials. Kids relate to their love of organizing and discovering patterns.

### 1.3 The Relationship Between Factions

The Yan and Yuan tribes are **not at war**. They are more like two siblings arguing over the best way to build a treehouse. The conflict is gentle and productive:

- **The Great Forge Contest (大炉赛):** Periodically, the tribes hold friendly competitions to see who can create the most amazing artifact. Children can participate and help their chosen tribe.
- **Exchange Programs:** Young members of each tribe sometimes visit the other to learn their techniques. This is how players unlock hybrid crafting recipes.
- **The Shared Threat:** Both tribes agree on one thing — the world is slowly losing its magic. Fragments of realms are fading into grey nothingness. They need each other's approaches to save it.

**Why this matters for children:**
- The factions model healthy disagreement — different approaches, mutual respect, collaboration when it matters.
- Children can identify with either tribe (or both!) without being forced to "pick a side" permanently.
- The game rewards learning from both philosophies.

## 2. The World of Shanhai (山海世界)

### 2.1 Cosmology: The Five Realms

The world is structured as **Five Realms (五境)**, each representing a different aspect of nature and myth. The realms are connected by the **World River (川)**, a magical waterway that flows between all of them. Players travel by riding on the backs of great creatures.

| Realm | Chinese | Theme | Description |
|-------|---------|-------|-------------|
| **Mountain Realm** | 山境 | Earth & Stone | Towering peaks where ancient creatures sleep in rock. Home to the Yan tribe's volcanic forges. |
| **Sea Realm** | 海境 | Water & Depth | Endless oceans with floating islands, underwater crystal caves, and the Yuan tribe's coral cities. |
| **Forest Realm** | 林境 | Life & Growth | A vast, ever-changing forest where plants grow overnight and trees can walk. Full of herbal ingredients and animal spirits. |
| **Cloud Realm** | 云境 | Sky & Wind | Floating islands among the clouds, where weather is crafted and storms are born. Home to flying creatures. |
| **Dream Realm** | 梦境 | Imagination & Mystery | A shifting, surreal realm that changes based on what children imagine. The rarest materials are found here. |

### 2.2 Key Locations Within Each Realm

**Mountain Realm (山境):**
- **不周山 (Buzhou Mountain)** — The legendary broken pillar of heaven. A massive mountain with a crack at its peak that leaks starlight. Children can climb it and find rare meteorite materials.
- **熔炉谷 (Forge Valley)** — The Yan tribe's homeland. Lava rivers, obsidian cliffs, and hundreds of forges of all sizes.
- **石语洞 (Stone-Whisper Cave)** — A cave system where the walls echo with the voices of ancient crafters. Solving echo-puzzles reveals hidden chambers.

**Sea Realm (海境):**
- **归墟 (Guixu / The Great Maelstrom)** — A massive whirlpool that is actually a portal to the Dream Realm. Children must navigate carefully.
- **珊瑚城 (Coral City)** — The Yuan tribe's capital, built from living coral that grows and changes shape. Streets rearrange themselves.
- **龙宫遗迹 (Dragon Palace Ruins)** — An underwater ruin filled with puzzles and ancient crafting knowledge.

**Forest Realm (林境):**
- **建木 (Jianmu / The World Tree)** — A colossal tree connecting all realms. Its branches hold villages, and its roots drink from the World River.
- **百草园 (Hundred Herbs Garden)** — A magical garden where every plant has unique properties. Children can grow and cross-breed plants.
- **迷踪林 (Lost Trail Forest)** — A forest that rearranges its paths. Only by leaving markers or using special compasses can children navigate.

**Cloud Realm (云境):**
- **风伯台 (Wind Lord's Platform)** — Where children learn to harness wind for crafting. They can create flying machines and gliders.
- **雷池 (Thunder Pool)** — A floating lake of liquid lightning. Dangerous but holds the most powerful energy materials.
- **彩虹桥 (Rainbow Bridge)** — A bridge that only appears when certain weather conditions are met. Leads to secret areas.

**Dream Realm (梦境):**
- **万象殿 (Hall of Ten Thousand Forms)** — A grand hall where every statue is a creature waiting to be awakened.
- **回音原 (Echo Plains)** — A vast plain where children's thoughts manifest as temporary objects. Great for experimentation.
- **遗忘之角 (Corner of Forgetting)** — Where faded realm fragments drift. Children can restore them using materials they've collected.

### 2.3 The World Feels Alive Through:

- **Day/Night & Seasonal Cycles:** The world shifts visually and mechanically. Certain creatures only appear at night. Certain materials can only be gathered in specific seasons.
- **Creature Ecology:** 山海经 creatures live, migrate, breed, and interact. Children can observe behaviors — a 九尾狐 (nine-tailed fox) might lead you to treasure if you're kind to it.
- **Dynamic Events:** Meteor showers, coral blooms, tree migrations, cloud storms — the world surprises you.
- **NPC Schedules:** Characters have daily routines. Grandma Yan might be at the forge in the morning, telling stories by the fire at night.
- **Player Impact:** The world remembers what children do. Plant a seed, come back later — it's a tree. Restore a faded fragment — creatures return to live there.

## 3. World Absorption Mechanic — "灵摄" (Spirit Capture)

This is the game's most unique feature: children can use a camera to photograph real-world objects, and those objects are **absorbed into the game world** as new materials, creatures, or elements.

### 3.1 The Narrative Frame

In the world of Shanhai, there exists an ancient artifact called the **万象镜 (Mirror of Ten Thousand Forms)**. Long ago, the first crafters created it to capture the essence of everything in existence. But it shattered into pieces, and its power went dormant.

When the child player first arrives in the world, the mirror's remaining fragment recognizes them as its new **Keeper (持镜人)**. The mirror can "see" the real world through the camera and absorb the **spirit essence (灵)** of whatever it photographs.

The narrative pitch to the child:

> *"You hold a piece of the oldest magic — a mirror that can bring the whole world into Shanhai. Every leaf, every stone, every creature you show it becomes part of this world. You are the bridge between your world and ours."*

### 3.2 How It Works (Player Experience)

1. **Open the Mirror:** The child opens the in-game 万象镜 interface, which activates the device's camera with a beautiful magical overlay (shimmering frame, ancient pattern border).
2. **Photograph:** Point at something in the real world and take a photo.
3. **Capture Animation:** A magical animation plays — the image gets "pulled" into the mirror with sparkling particles, like the object's spirit is being drawn through glass.
4. **Analysis:** The mirror "reads" the captured essence and identifies what it is. A playful spirit guide pops up and says things like:
   - *"Ooh, a green leaf! This is... let me think... grass energy! The Yan tribe would love this for their herbal alloys!"*
   - *"A pinecone! This has Wood essence with a hint of Earth. The Yuan tribe would know exactly what to do with this."*
5. **Reward:** The child receives crafting materials, new recipes, or even creature encounters based on what they captured.

### 3.3 Absorption Categories

The game classifies captured objects into **Five Essence Types (五灵)**, each corresponding to a realm:

| Essence Type | Chinese | Real-World Examples | In-Game Result |
|-------------|---------|-------------------|----------------|
| **Earth** | 土灵 | Rocks, soil, sand, pottery, bricks | Stone materials, mineral recipes, mountain realm unlocks |
| **Wood** | 木灵 | Leaves, flowers, bark, paper, wooden objects | Plant materials, herbal recipes, forest realm unlocks |
| **Water** | 水灵 | Water (puddles, cups), ice, mirrors, glass | Liquid materials, crystal recipes, sea realm unlocks |
| **Fire** | 火灵 | Candles, warm-colored objects, sunlight (safe!), red items | Energy materials, forge boosters, cloud realm unlocks |
| **Metal** | 金灵 | Coins, keys, jewelry, tools, electronics | Metal materials, advanced recipes, dream realm unlocks |

**Special Categories:**
- **Living Essence (生灵):** Photographing animals, insects, or pets can summon 山海经 creatures or reveal creature-related recipes. A photo of a family cat might summon a 狸力 (a mythical creature that looks like a cat).
- **Human Essence (人灵):** Photographing drawings, toys, or family photos can create unique "memory artifacts" — one-of-a-kind items imbued with personal meaning.
- **Weather Essence (天气灵):** Photographing rain, snow, rainbows, or clouds unlocks weather-based crafting recipes.

### 3.4 Progression & Rarity

Not all captures are equal. The mirror has a **spirit resonance level**:

- **Common (凡):** Everyday objects — leaves, rocks, water. Give basic materials.
- **Uncommon (奇):** Interesting objects — unusual flowers, interesting rocks, animal tracks. Give special materials.
- **Rare (珍):** Rare objects — a four-leaf clover, a unique insect, a beautiful sunset. Give rare recipes and realm keys.
- **Legendary (神):** Extraordinary captures — a rainbow, a meteor, a once-in-a-lifetime moment. Unlock entire new areas or summon legendary 山海经 creatures.

The mirror levels up as children capture more diverse objects. A higher-level mirror can detect rarer essences and unlock more powerful recipes.

### 3.5 Why This Is Magical for Children

- **The whole world becomes a treasure hunt.** A walk in the park becomes an expedition. Even the backyard holds secrets.
- **Personal connection.** The materials in their game world are literally from *their* world. A sword forged from "the leaf from my backyard" means something.
- **Encourages exploration and observation.** Children learn to look closely at the world around them.
- **Educational value.** The mirror teaches children about natural materials, categories, and properties through play.
- **Unlimited content.** Every child's world is different. A child in the city captures different things than a child in the countryside — and both experiences are equally valid and rich.

### 3.6 Safety & Practical Considerations

- **Parental controls** over camera access and location data.
- **No facial recognition or storage of photos.** Images are processed locally and immediately discarded after essence extraction.
- **Encourages safe exploration.** The game never asks children to go to dangerous places.
- **Offline-friendly.** The mirror works without internet. Processing happens on-device.

## 4. Story & Narrative Hooks

### 4.1 The Overarching Story: "The Fading"

Long ago, the world of Shanhai was vibrant and whole. The Five Realms were connected by the World River, and the Mirror of Ten Thousand Forms held the essence of every living thing. The Yan and Yuan tribes worked together, combining fire and water, change and patience, to maintain the world's magic.

But then came **The Fading (黯褪)** — a mysterious force that began draining color, life, and magic from the edges of the world. Entire fragments of realms broke off and floated into nothingness, becoming grey, lifeless voids. Creatures lost their powers. Materials lost their spirit. The World River slowed to a trickle.

The two tribes blamed each other at first. The Yan said the Yuan were too passive — they should have acted faster. The Yuan said the Yan were too reckless — their experiments might have caused it. They split apart, each trying to solve the problem their own way. Neither approach worked alone.

Now, the Fading is accelerating. And the only hope is a **new Keeper of the Mirror** — a child from the world beyond Shanhai, someone who can bring fresh essence from a world that Shanhai has never seen before.

**That child is the player.**

### 4.2 What Motivates Children to Play

**Short-term motivations:**
- **Discovery:** What's around the next corner? What creature lives here? What happens if I combine these two materials?
- **Collection:** Filling the Mirror's encyclopedia with new essences. Completing creature albums. Finding every recipe.
- **Creation:** Making cool things. Showing off unique artifacts to friends. "I made this from a photo of my dog!"
- **Helping:** NPCs give quests — a creature needs a new home, a village needs protection, a fading fragment needs restoring.

**Medium-term motivations:**
- **Realm restoration:** Each realm has a "vitality meter." As children complete quests, capture essences, and craft artifacts, the realm becomes more colorful and alive. Watching a grey, faded area burst back into color is deeply satisfying.
- **Friendship:** Building relationships with creatures and NPCs. A rescued 九尾狐 becomes a companion. A befriended village elder shares ancient secrets.
- **Faction reputation:** Helping the Yan or Yuan tribe unlocks unique abilities and recipes. Balancing both tribes' trust unlocks the most powerful hybrid techniques.

**Long-term motivations:**
- **Saving the world:** The ultimate goal is to stop The Fading and reconnect all Five Realms. This is a journey that unfolds over many play sessions.
- **The truth of The Fading:** As children progress, they discover clues about what The Fading really is. (Spoiler: it's not a villain — it's the world's way of asking to be remembered. The Faking happens when people stop exploring, stop creating, stop noticing the magic in everyday things. The cure is exactly what the child is doing.)
- **Personal legacy:** The world the child creates is unique to them. No two players will have the same Shanhai world, because no two players will have captured the same real-world objects.

### 4.3 The Child's Role: The Keeper (持镜人)

The child is not a warrior or a chosen-one prophecy figure. They are something more relatable and powerful:

**They are a Creator.** Their tools are curiosity, imagination, and the Mirror. They solve problems by understanding the world, gathering materials, and making things that help others.

**They are a Bridge.** Between the real world and Shanhai. Between the Yan and Yuan tribes. Between the Five Realms. Between the past (ancient crafting knowledge) and the future (new discoveries).

**They are a Friend.** To creatures, to NPCs, to the world itself. Many quests are solved through kindness and understanding rather than combat.

### 4.4 Story Beats (Narrative Arc)

| Act | Title | What Happens |
|-----|-------|-------------|
| **Act 1** | The Shattered Mirror | Child discovers the Mirror fragment, enters Shanhai, meets both tribes, and learns about The Fading. Tutorial zone. |
| **Act 2** | First Sparks | Child explores the Mountain and Sea Realms, learns basic crafting from both tribes, and restores the first fading fragments. |
| **Act 3** | The Living World | Forest and Cloud Realms open. Child discovers creature companionship and advanced hybrid crafting. The Fading worsens — a whole village is lost. |
| **Act 4** | The Truth | Child reaches the Dream Realm and discovers the true nature of The Faking. The Mirror reveals its full power. The tribes must work together. |
| **Act 5** | The Great Forge | Child leads a massive collaborative effort — Yan and Yuan unite, all Five Realms contribute, and the child uses everything they've learned to create the **Ultimate Artifact** that reconnects the world. |
| **Epilogue** | A New World | The world is saved — but it's changed. It's now a blend of Shanhai and the child's real world. New adventures await. The story continues with seasonal content. |

### 4.5 Narrative Tone

- **Warm and wondrous**, never dark or scary. Even The Fading is presented as a mystery to solve, not a horror to fear.
- **Humorous.** Characters are quirky and funny. Grandma Yan's explosions, Grandpa Yuan's long stories, creature antics.
- **Respectful of children's intelligence.** The story doesn't talk down. Themes of environmentalism, cooperation, creativity, and cultural heritage are woven in naturally.
- **Rooted in Chinese mythology but accessible.** 山海经 creatures and places are presented with wonder and respect. Children learn about this cultural heritage organically through play.
- **Personal.** The child's real-world experiences become part of the story. Their backyard, their pet, their drawings — all matter in Shanhai.

## Appendix: Key Design Principles

1. **Every child's world is unique.** The photo absorption mechanic ensures no two play experiences are identical.
2. **Cooperation over conflict.** The factions model healthy disagreement and collaboration.
3. **Exploration rewards curiosity.** The world is designed to make children want to look closer, try new things, and come back later.
4. **Cultural heritage through play.** 山海经 mythology is woven into every aspect — creatures, locations, crafting techniques, stories — making it a natural learning experience.
5. **The real world matters.** By connecting the game world to the child's physical environment, we encourage outdoor play, observation, and appreciation of nature.
